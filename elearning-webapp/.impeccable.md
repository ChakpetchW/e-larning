## Design Context

### Users
This product is designed for employees inside organizations and for HR/L&D teams that manage learning programs.
It should work well for organizations from roughly 50 users up to 1,000+ users.
The product should feel ready for white-label or B2B enterprise sales, not like a prototype.

### Brand Personality
- ใช้งานง่าย
- สวย
- แสดงผลได้เป๊ะทุกอุปกรณ์

The product should feel premium, trustworthy, modern, and polished for Thai enterprise buyers.
It can feel bold and impressive, but it should not feel noisy, gimmicky, or like a generic AI template.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Reference mood: FutureSkill, Skooldio, SkillLane
- Light-first visual system
- Premium enterprise presentation
- Strong clarity and hierarchy
- Thai-market appeal with polished visual energy

### Design Principles
1. Clarity before flair. Every screen should be easy to scan and understand quickly.
2. Premium, but practical. Use gradients, motion, and depth to elevate, not distract.
3. Responsive without compromise. Core layouts must feel intentional on phone, tablet, and desktop.
4. Enterprise trust matters. UI should look reliable enough for demos, procurement, and daily internal use.
5. Thai-friendly polish. Typography, spacing, and visual density should feel right for Thai users.
6. Mobile touch targets must feel generous. Pills, tabs, filters, and icon buttons should usually be at least 44-48px tall on phones.
7. Horizontal mobile category bars should use pill-like tap targets with enough padding for easy one-handed use while scrolling.
8. Equal-height card rows are required in mobile carousels. Cards in the same horizontal row should align visually and avoid height jitter from uneven text blocks.
